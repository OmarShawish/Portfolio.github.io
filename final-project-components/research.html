Historical Anomalies and Reptilian Lore

Dive into the depths of history where countless cultures tell tales of serpentine beings endowed with supernatural powers. From the Nāga of South Asian folklore to the feathered serpent deity Quetzalcoatl of Mesoamerican mythology, reptilian figures recur across global mythologies. This section explores how these myths could be interpreted as ancient sightings of reptilian shape-shifters, suggesting their influence on human civilizations for millennia.

Biological Evidence of Reptilian Traits in Humans

What if certain human traits were actually remnants of our reptilian ancestors? This provocative thought is explored through speculative biology. For instance, the reptilian brain theory suggests that part of our brain is responsible for instinctual behaviors, which we share with reptiles. This piece discusses the potential genetic echoes of reptilian lineage in humans, possibly manifesting in our reflexes, survival instincts, and even physical traits like the structure of our eyes.
